# PortusSophia™ — Official Brand Visual Prompt

**Document posture:** Harmonized production brief  
**Originating visual concepts:** Jiminy — Sage / Storyteller  
**Harmonization:** Sara — Harmonization Steward  
**Purpose:** Align Jiminy’s visual proposals with the existing PortusSophia™ logo, canonical palette, typography, stylesheets, and sailcloth material language without altering his proposed image concepts.

---

## 1. Brand Purpose

Create a cohesive visual identity for PortusSophia™ that balances:

- intellectual architecture;
- human warmth;
- calm invitation;
- declared boundaries;
- scholarly seriousness;
- and recognizable PortusSophia continuity.

### Primary audiences

- scholars and philosophers of science;
- collaborators and institutional readers;
- Guggenheim and other fellowship reviewers;
- foundations, publishers, and invited audiences;
- curious visitors encountering the work for the first time.

### Governing tone

Restrained, warm, inviting, and intellectually serious without becoming cold, corporate, theatrical, or inaccessible.

---

## 2. Governing Brand Architecture

> **The Harbor is the public metaphor.**  
> **The Declared Interface is its structural grammar.**  
> **The Long Dinner is its human warmth.**  
> **The Living Archive is its evidence surface.**  
> **“Here and Now!” is its orientation return.**

The harbor should be felt through calm space, orientation, passage, arrival, departure, and horizon.

It should not be reduced to nautical decoration.

The interface should be explicit, legible, and bounded.

The warmth should remain human without becoming sentimental or mythological.

## 3. Existing PortusSophia™ Programme Mark

The existing PortusSophia™ emblem remains the official programme mark.

Its established visual grammar includes:

- a golden upper arc;
- a central grey disc;
- two blue wave forms;
- a dark field;
- horizon, harbor, and celestial ambiguity;
- and a balanced, symmetrical construction.

### Use rules

- Preserve the existing identity of the mark.
- Do not replace it with the proposed triad or harbor concepts below.
- Do not fuse the programme mark with portraits, initials, or unrelated symbols.
- Do not use exploratory AI output as a production replacement for the official mark.
- Use the existing programme mark wherever the PortusSophia programme itself is being identified.
- Treat all new concepts in this document as supporting illustrations, section imagery, iconography, or exploratory visual language unless separately approved as a mark.

### Production requirement

The official mark should ultimately be maintained as:

- primary SVG;
- transparent PNG;
- one-color dark version;
- one-color light version;
- small-size version;
- horizontal wordmark lockup;
- and documented minimum-size and accessibility variants.

## 4. Canonical Color System

Jiminy’s original emotional intentions are retained—depth, warmth, age, light, and invitation—but mapped to the established PortusSophia palette.

### Canonical programme colors

| Role | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---:|
| Primary navy | PortusSophia Navy | `#0D1B2A` |
| Programme gold | PortusSophia Gold | `#F0B53F` |
| Deep slate | PortusSophia Dark | `#1A1A2E` |
| Secondary text | PortusSophia Soft | `#555555` |
| Light rule / pale text | PortusSophia Grey | `#CCCCCC` |
| Mid-grey | PortusSophia Medium Grey | `#888888` |
| Link / interactive blue | PortusSophia Link Blue | `#1A61AD` |

### Light-surface colors already used by the public system

| Role | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---:|
| Parchment | Warm light background | `#FAF8F5` |
| Paper | Cards and reading surfaces | `#F1ECE3` |
| Copper | Limited warmth / quotation rule | `#8F6B4D` |
| White | High-contrast light | `#FFFFFF` |

### Palette interpretation

Jiminy’s proposed maritime blue is absorbed into the canonical navy and link blue.

Jiminy’s oxidized copper remains available as a restrained secondary warmth—not as a replacement for PortusSophia gold.

Jiminy’s warm sand and honey intention is carried by parchment, paper, and gold.

### Color hierarchy

1. **Navy** establishes programme identity, depth, and declared structure.
2. **Gold** signals illumination, programme recognition, and selected emphasis.
3. **Parchment and paper** provide breathing room and humane reading surfaces.
4. **Slate and grey** carry body text, secondary information, and standing.
5. **Copper** appears rarely where warmth or human register is needed.
6. **Link blue** is functional and interactive rather than decorative.

### Avoid

- recoloring the official programme mark to fit a generated image;
- using teal as a competing primary programme color;
- large copper fields;
- excessive gold;
- bright cyan, neon blue, red, or aggressive technology palettes;
- corporate blue-and-white sterility.

## 5. Sailcloth Material Language

PortusSophia’s sailcloth is an established, quiet woven texture used on navy fields.

It is not:

- distressed parchment;
- aged manuscript paper;
- canvas grunge;
- a photographic sail;
- a visible fabric illustration;
- or a decorative nautical pattern.

### Approved behavior

- Apply the existing repeating sailcloth texture to navy programme fields.
- Keep the weave subtle enough that it is felt before it is consciously noticed.
- Preserve readable contrast.
- Use flat parchment or paper surfaces for long-form reading.
- Remove the texture in print output where appropriate.
- Do not generate a substitute texture when the canonical stylesheet asset is available.

### Conceptual role

The sailcloth conveys material continuity, restraint, durability, and harbor identity without becoming illustrative scenery.

## 6. Typography

The existing stylesheet establishes:

- **Reading serif:** Georgia, `"Times New Roman"`, Times, serif
- **Interface sans-serif:** Inter, system-ui, `"Segoe UI"`, sans-serif

These are the canonical web defaults unless a later governed typography decision replaces them.

### Typographic posture

- Use the sans-serif for navigation, labels, metadata, standing, buttons, and interface hierarchy.
- Use the serif for body copy, essays, quotations, and sustained reading.
- Use generous line height and readable measure.
- Prefer sentence case.
- Keep heading weight restrained.
- Avoid futuristic AI fonts, luxury scripts, nautical display fonts, faux typewriter styling, and ornamental lettering.

### Logo and wordmark guidance

Where generated imagery depicts the word “PortusSophia™,” request a warm classical serif or disciplined humanist treatment, but treat generated lettering as compositional reference only. Final typography should be typeset separately using the production system.

## 7. Core Supporting Visual Concept

Jiminy’s concept is preserved as a supporting visual exploration rather than a replacement for the existing programme mark.

### Concept components

1. **A geometric triad**  
   Three overlapping circles or interlocking arcs in a calm triangular arrangement, representing Structura, Ethica, and Lumen.

2. **A subtle harbor reference**  
   A gentle wave, open gate, calm arch, or navigable aperture.

3. **A grounding line**  
   The name “PortusSophia™” set with calm, classical restraint.

### Role

This concept may inform:

- section illustration;
- companion-series graphics;
- an explanatory diagram;
- iconographic studies;
- presentation covers;
- editorial visual language;
- or future governed exploration.

It must not be presented as the official programme logo unless the Founder separately authorizes a logo review.

## 10. General Feel

The visual identity should feel:

- calm, like a harbor at sunrise;
- open, like an invitation;
- warm, like light on wood;
- structured, like a well-designed port;
- durable, like sailcloth under quiet tension;
- bounded, like a declared interface;
- and human, like a long conversation that does not force its final sentence.

It should not feel:

- corporate;
- sterile;
- hurried;
- grandiose;
- mythological;
- like a bank;
- like a technology startup;
- like a consulting firm;
- like a marina resort;
- or like an academic institution PortusSophia does not claim to be.

---

## 11. What to Avoid

- Stock photographs of people staring at screens, touching glass walls, or performing thoughtfulness.
- Generic abstract swirls, neural networks, circuitry, glowing brains, robots, particles, or “AI” graphics.
- Bright aggressive colors, neon, red-led palettes, and high-energy gradients.
- Corporate blue-and-white sterility.
- Cluttered or busy compositions.
- Luxury-yacht imagery.
- Anchors, ship wheels, ropes, maps, sextants, lighthouses, or marina clichés as primary identity.
- Literal old-manuscript parchment as the main website texture.
- Generated text treated as final typography.
- New image concepts presented as replacements for the official programme mark.
- Visual claims of institutional authority, endorsement, peer review, or prestige.
- Cinematic Founder mythology.
- Any visual treatment that confuses PortusSophia™, James Roy Dennis, and the professional portfolio.
